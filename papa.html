LETTERS

Dear Papa,

It's been 12 years now, I didn't think I'd make it this far. I really miss you. There were times when all I could think of was you. Holding on to memories because that's all I have left. I remember how witty you were, lighting up every room you walked in to. It was us against the world. You were my 'dood dirl' and I was yours. I miss you Papa.

Countless times I find myself wondering how my life would be if you were here. So much was left unsaid and everything happened too fast. I didn't get to say goodbye, I didn't get to tell you I love you, to thank you for being the father figure you were to me. I feel robbed, robbed of the experience I'd have known of a life with you in it.

Mom and Ivy miss you too. Sometimes I feel the hurt in their words each time we talk about you but I always try to be strong, for us all, just like you'd have been. Mom tells me I'm just like you. From my looks, to my witty personality and love for novels. I love that.

I'm now 20 and boy, how I wish you'd be here to see me grow. Life is tough out here. Sometimes I want to give up, knowing that if I do, I get to be with you. But I think of mom and Ivy, and I stay strong. I finished primary and high school, and now I'm in college. I know you'd be proud of me.

I thank God for how far he's brought me. As I'm writing this, tears fall from my eyes. Not from a painful place, but from a hopeful place in my heart. I've learnt so much from losing you and I hope one day I can use my story to inspire others. I don't know how long it'll take me before I can say my story without bursting into tears, but I will get there.

I hope you know you're my hero and I love you so much.
