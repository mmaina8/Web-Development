GIFTED

I ran all the way home right after school. I had had enough, I had seen enough and I didn't know what was going on. 'Hey dear,' my mom called out once I barged in. 'Hey mom' I shouted back. I locked myself in my room, shoved my head in my pillow and sobbed. Things had not been okay.

It started three weeks ago when I was walking home from school. It all felt too familiar. I knew things were going to happen right when they did. I knew that a cat would run across the road right when it did, I knew I'd meet Ms. Johnson right when I did and I knew she was going to ask me about my mom. It's like I was experiencing everything all over again.

I decided to ignore it and went on with my days as usual. But it didn't stop. Things got worse. I was literally living already knowing what would happen in the future. I didn't talk to anyone about it because I knew this wasn't just normal behavior. I knew that I'd be judged, that I'd lose all my friends. This was my problem and I had to deal with it on my own. Or so I thought. I woke up one day restless and tired. I had been tossing and turning the whole night because my visions wouldn't let me sleep. This time, they weren't the usual ones.

I had visions about my mom, and they were horrifying. In an instance, I jumped out of bed and ran downstairs. 'Mom!' I called out. When no one answered, I immediately fell to my knees. Had my visions come true? Was I too late to save my mom? I slowly crawled to the kitchen and stood there, dumbfounded at what was in front of me.

My worst nightmare had come true.
